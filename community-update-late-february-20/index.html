Community Update - Late February '20

The end of February included the first implementation of the execution economy within the MESG Engine, plus we updated the Explorer, and launched a new 2-month long online hackathon.

  • Will Morgan

    Brand Manager @MESG

    More posts by Will Morgan.

Will Morgan

2 Mar 2020 • 1 min read

Research & Development

  • Implemented the first iteration of the execution economy in the Engine
  • Updated the Engine to Cosmos v0.38 and Tendermint v0.33
  • Released a new Product Update
  • Added the ERC-20 to Webhook process in the Explorer
  • Fixed some issues on Tendermint
  • Updated the Explorer based on Tendermint’s new version
  • Worked on Milestone 2 for the Aragon/MESG integration
  • Started an Ethereum service on our local node
  • Worked Engine architecture schemas
  • Updated the quick start documentation
  • Created a service process deployer

Marketing & Communications

  • Prepared for launch of our second hackathon on Devpost
  • Released Token Utility in the MESG Economy Article
  • Released the MESG Foundation’s February Financial Report
  • Created a MESG Economy Forum Group
  • Expanded the MonetizeYourCode collaborative resource list
  • Integrated a new social and newsletter section on our blog posts ⬇️
  • Released a new comic for MonetizeYourCode
  • Continued marketing growth campaigns
  • Worked on new outreach strategies for targeted A/B testing
  • Updated visual design of the Explorer
  • Added new MESG stickers to the Telegram community. Thanks to contributor Afif Ridwan!

Partners & Administration

  • Began discussions with Lunie to have MESG integrated into their platform.
  • Worked with Devpost to throw frequent hackathons

Stay up to date

To stay up to date on MESG’s latest developments, join our Forum or chat with us on Discord. Advanced users, head to Github. Plus, if you have suggestions or improvements, feel free to make a pull request. MESG is open source and community-driven.
